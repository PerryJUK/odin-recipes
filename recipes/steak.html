The taste is to die for

Ever since we bought our Traeger wood pellet grill last year, we have gotten a lot of use out of it. We have made brisket, pulled pork, nachos, burgers and more and everything has been amazing.

Once you start smoking meat, it is hard to eat it any other way! A couple of weeks ago, we were invited to a Super Bowl party. We offered to bring a few things, including some wings. We thought about making our delicious buffalo wings recipe but decided that we wanted to try something new instead. In the end, we decided to try our hand at making smoked chicken wings and they were delicious.

Ingredients

  • 16 Chicken Wings
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp Treager Chicken Rub
  • 1 tbsp Traeger Rub
  • 1 cup Traeger BBQ sauce

Instructions

  1. Add the chicken wings to a large ziplock bag and add the olive oil and rubs. Shake around until wings are coated with oil and rub.
  2. Toss the wings in the BBQ sauce and increase heat to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  3. Add wings to the wood pellet grill and cook for about 45 minutes, or until wings reach an internal temperature of 160 degrees Fahrenheit.
  4. Return wings to the grill and cook for an additional 20 minutes.
  5. Instructions Serve with ranch dressing or alone.
  6. Preheat wood pellet grill to 225 degrees Fahrenheit.
